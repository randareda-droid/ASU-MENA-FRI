What is ASU-MENA-FRI?

🌟 Overview

The Ain Shams University–Middle East North Africa FAIMER Regional Institute (ASU-MENA-FRI) is a premier regional fellowship based at the Faculty of Medicine, Ain Shams University, Cairo, Egypt. Established in 2019, it is the only FAIMER Regional Institute serving the entire MENA region.

The program focuses on health professions education, leadership development, and community building. Its curriculum is based on the International FAIMER Institute (USA) and adapted to the specific needs and context of health professions educators across the Middle East and North Africa.

To date, over 240 fellows from the MENA region and beyond have graduated, contributing to a growing body of health professions educators well-versed in best practices of teaching and learning.

🎯 Vision

To initiate an exchange of best practices in the Middle East and North Africa by attracting change agents capable of leading medical education reform in relevance to their national and community needs.

🚀 Mission

To provide a collaborative environment for scholars in leadership positions in health professions education across MENA countries, ensuring they gain up-to-date knowledge, skills, and attitudes that help them lead change through communities of practice.

About FAIMER

🌐 The FAIMER Global Network

The Foundation for Advancement of International Medical Education and Research (FAIMER) was established in 2000 by the Educational Commission for Foreign Medical Graduates (ECFMG) in Philadelphia, USA. FAIMER concentrates on improving health professions education in developing regions through faculty development, targeted research, and educational quality improvement.

There are 10 FAIMER Regional Institutes worldwide — one in Egypt, four in India, two in China, one in Chile, one in Indonesia, and one in Uganda. The ASU-MENA-FRI is the exclusive institute for the Middle East and North Africa region.

✅
The ASU-MENA-FRI certificate is accredited by both Ain Shams University (ASU) and the International FAIMER Institute (IFI) in Philadelphia. All FAIMER certificates — whether from regional institutes or the international institute — are held with equal distinction and prestige worldwide.

Program Structure

📐 Two-Year Part-Time Fellowship

The ASU-MENA-FRI is a two-year, part-time program designed to be completed alongside your existing professional role. Fellows focus on developing, implementing, evaluating, and disseminating an educational intervention project, supported by a core didactic curriculum delivered across four sessions.

Year 1 · Sessions 1 & 2
Project Development & Early Implementation
Fellows develop their educational intervention project with guidance from a dedicated Project Advisor and peer cohort. During this phase, fellows seek ethical approval (where needed) and begin the implementation of their change project at their home institution. One session is held face-to-face in Cairo; the other is online.
Year 2 · Sessions 3 & 4
Evaluation, Dissemination & Project Report
Fellows continue full implementation while focusing on evaluating outcomes and disseminating their findings. Outputs include a formal Project Report and/or a conference abstract. A second face-to-face session in Cairo and a final online session complete the program journey.
Graduation
Dual Accreditation & Global Community Membership
Graduates receive a certificate accredited by Ain Shams University and the International FAIMER Institute in Philadelphia. Fellows join a lifelong, worldwide community of health professions educators. The fellowship journey never truly ends.

💻 Online Sessions (×2)
  • Synchronous and asynchronous distance learning
  • Flexible participation from your home country
  • Continued project mentoring and peer exchange
  • Access to global FAIMER online resources

Core Curriculum

📚 What You Will Learn

The curriculum is based on the International FAIMER Institute's framework, modified to be conceptual and relevant to the local needs of MENA health professions educators. It covers:

Curriculum Domain Key Skills & Outcomes
Educational Methods Curriculum design, teaching strategies, student assessment, clinical education
Leadership & Management Leading institutional change, team management, strategic planning, stakeholder engagement
Scholarship & Research Educational research design, ethical approval, data collection and analysis, abstract writing
Project Implementation Project planning, timelines, goal-setting, sustainability strategies, institutionalization
Community of Practice Peer mentoring, interprofessional collaboration, dissemination, network building
🌟
The MENA region contributes to the dissemination of advanced educational methods in health professions, creating a growing body of educators and increasing the number of health professions institutions practicing evidence-based teaching and learning.

  • The program is open to health professionals from the Middle East and North Africa
  • Applicants from other Asian countries may also be considered
  • Approximately 35 fellows are accepted into each annual cohort
🏥 Professional Profile
  • Health professionals from all disciplines working in health professions education
  • Targeting academic leaders in positions that facilitate implementation of change

  • Those with the potential to play key roles in improving education at their institutions

Scholarships & Fees

💰 Financial Support

The ASU-MENA-FRI offers a wide range of scholarships for different nationalities, based on country economic status and the level of institutional support offered by the applicant's home institution.

Scholarship options are communicated to applicants upon acceptance of their project proposal — you do not need to worry about fees before you are accepted.

Tuition fees are calculated according to the currency exchange rate at the time of registration. For specific fee inquiries before applying, please contact the program directly.

💡
Even if you are uncertain about fees, do not let this stop you from applying. The scholarship range is broad and is designed to be accessible to health professionals across the diverse economic landscape of the MENA region.

Application Steps

Step 1
Define Your Project Idea
Identify an educational problem or gap at your institution that you want to address. Think about a realistic, implementable intervention — this becomes the heart of your fellowship experience. Your project should be something you have the position and authority to change.
Step 2
Obtain Institutional Support & Nomination
Secure a formal nomination from your institution's leadership (Dean, Department Chair, or equivalent). This demonstrates your institution's commitment to supporting your participation and backing your project. This is a mandatory requirement.
Step 3
Complete the Online Application
Visit the official FAIMER application portal and complete your application form with all required supporting documentation. Ensure all sections are thoroughly filled and submitted before the deadline. Late submissions are not reviewed.
Step 4
Review Process
All submitted applications are reviewed after the submission deadline closes. The review committee evaluates your project proposal, institutional support, and professional profile. Results are communicated by email, typically by mid-September.
Step 5
Acceptance & Scholarship Options
Accepted fellows receive an offer letter along with details of available scholarship options tailored to their nationality and institutional context. You then confirm your participation and complete registration with the applicable fee.
⚠️
The review procedure starts only after the submission deadline. Make sure your application is complete and submitted well before the closing date. Check the FAIMER portal regularly for deadline updates.
